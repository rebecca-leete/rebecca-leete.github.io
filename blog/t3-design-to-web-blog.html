Getting my hands dirty

Responsive sites re-visited

We touched on repsonsive websites in the last blog post, talking about how it's important to have a webpage that can be re-sized for different screen sizes. It provides pages that are easy and intuitive to navigate, quick to load, and they're designed to look great! This can make or break a site in terms of traffic and engagement - never underestimate ease-of-use as a tool for success!

Mobile-first Design

Apparently over half of all the internet traffic worldwide now happens via mobile devices. That's huge, and it's only increasing. Having websites that cater to mobile device screens and interfac es are more important than ever, and will make a huge difference to who interacts with your site and how. Mobile-first design just means designing your site first and foremost for a mobile screen, and expanding from there for other sizes. It is much simpler and easier to design for a smaller screen first and "expand" that design for bigger screens than it is to try and collapse a page initially designed for larger screens.

Frameworks

Frameworks! Ah! So handy and helpful! Frameworks are these wonderful things people have designed to take (some of) the tedious parts out of building a website. It's kind of like doing a colour-by-number rather than doing a full painting from scratch. Frameworks vary in complexity from providing very simple or minimal frames for front-end design, like Skeleton, to vast frames including a huge variety of features for design, function and databases, like Ruby on Rails. They're great in the way that they can make web design much easier and more streamlined, but the downside is that it limits your understanding of how your website is built and behaves, and if something goes wrong, it's a lot of unfamiliar code to sort through in order to find the bug.

Wireframes

Wireframes are fun, they're the pages of scribbled squares that pile up right at the start the web design process. We looked at boxified design and grid-based design earlier, and wireframes are the visual representation of this design process. It's often quite difficult to keep the countless boxes-witin-boxes-within-boxes in our head, so spacing it out on paper ensures all of your divs, rows and columns. and the content within it, is going to fit together smoothly.
