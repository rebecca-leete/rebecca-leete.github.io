First Impressions

So I've finished my first sprint! How has it been? What do I feel like I've gotten myself into? A little look at Why I'm here and how my expectations have lined up with reality so far

Cultural Blog II

Emotional Intelligence

Success is not just about your practical skills and knowledge. And this is not just about success. This looks at the importance of understanding yourself and others, and being able to artfully hold the relationship between both of these understandings.

Cultural Blog III

Trusting the Process

Process is about patience. It's about trusting that the work you're doing is going to pay off in the long term, instead of focussing on great results short-term. I really like this because I think it reflects a really healthy attitude to life in general, and one that I do my best to practice: placing my self-worth in my efforts and intention rather than my outcomes. Because we're always learning!

Cultural Blog IV

Making Every Day Mindful

I've known about mindfulness for a while. But really, I am only just starting to understand how it can unlock a person. Here I explore on how to integrate mindfulness into everyday life, and reflect on how it can change attitudes of success and failure, and consequently transform ideas of self-worth and ability. Search inside yourself indeed!

Cultural Blog V

Making Every Day Mindful

Self confidence is not about making yourself into such a great person that you trust youtrself to act desirably in any situation. It is about acknowledging and making peace with your insecurites. There's a Buddhist premise that says that we can only desire things we do not already have, and I think in that lies the secret path to all things of the self: confidence, love, trust, etc... In accepting ourselves the way we are, we are actually free to be the best version of ourselves. Wow!

Cultural Blog VI

Self Control

Like coding, it's one thing to cultivate good emotions - but it's another thing entirely to deal capably and calmly with problems when they arise. It's really interesting to explore the dark side of our emotional self through things like triggers, and if it's done from a place of stability and curiosity, it's definitely where the most kearning happens too.

Cultural Blog VII

Motivation

My favourite one so far!! Looking at motivation allows us to explore the deeper parts of ourselves, the grand and fantastical things that are in our hearts and our inner child. I am glad purpose was acknowledged in this chapter, as I see the sense purpose as possibly the most powerful source of motivation known to man, and it is often not explored fully because the modern-western world loves to involve itself in matters of the mind over matters of the heart. A thoroughly enriching exercise!
