Self Confidence

Mindfulness vs Self-awareness

I can absolutely understand the parallel drawn between mindfulness and self-awareness. They are both describing the practice of paying attention to the traffic of your mind and heart without judgement. Judgement tends to dissolve the clarity that comes with neutral observation, I find. I feel like self-awareness is almost the next level of mindfulness - like self-awareness is sustained mindfulness held in an embedded state of mind.

Journalling: the emotional coder's equivalent of pseudocoding

I have been journalling since the beginning of hig school as a wee 14 year-old, and I would without a doubt attribute a huge amount of my intrapersonal relationship to this practice. For me it's like... Having a concrete record of my train of thought, and somehow this seems to inherently create a wonderful amount of clarity. I solve so many problem just through the act of writing them down. It's kind of like pseudocoding in this way, and just provides a space where you can explore and process what is happening in life and inside without any fear of it actually creating any external impacts.
I haven't done consistent journalling in a while, but reading about the statistical benefits really encourages and reminds me to get back into it!

Me myself and My Emotions

I love the idea of stepping back from our emotions. I first heard about it in Buddhist lore, I think. However I think it's important to be careful with how we approach our emotions, because I think "process" can often be the very thing that stops us from processing our emotions. I find that if I take too much of a systemic approach to dealing with how I'm feeling often pulls me into my head and has me intellectualising my emotions, which in turn stops them from being "digested" in a healthy way. I can totally empathise with this because there are a lot of emotions that are really raw and difficult to confront honestly, but it's definitely definitely better in the long run.

My approach to my emotions is similar though, in the way that I don't identify with them, but I go a step further and make sure I still honour them. I try acknowledge what I'm feeling, with as little resistance as possible, and to hold space for it in the most instinctive and non-structured way possible. This allows me to fully process what I'm feeling without identifying with it, which in turn creates an excellent vantage point for observing what has caused, shaped and interacted with these feelings on a much deeper level. Being able to reach such insights into my own human behaviour allows me to recognise similar patterns in others, and enhances my sense of empathy and interbeing.

Yeah, I love this stuff.
