Motivation

A hierarchy of happiness

Pleasure, Passion and Purpose. Three doorways to happiness. This was cool to read as I often spend time observing and surrendering to my generation's addiction to instant gratification (pleasure), and then end up pep talking myself about how this addiction has been created by an absence of deeper happiness (and is now just habitual :( ). Looking at it from a Purpose-down perspective, this to me clearly sets people up for a healthier, more successful and more integrated life. I have often wondered if the matter purpose sits at the base of all happiness and unhappiness.

Core Values

Wow. I think this is my favourite chapter so far. This stuff is so important. I'm a very spiritual person, and see my time on earth as an opportunity to grow: physically, mentally, emotionally, spiritually. So to me, new experiences - both positive and negative - are deeply valued. So cultivating anf maintaining an open mind and open heart are both really really important to me. I also think the world is really fucking beautiful (excuse my french) on every level, and acknowledging and celebrating that feels really important as well.

What I stand for

I care deeply for the state of the world and the people in it. This means that I am pretty highly invested in making it a good place to exist, and prepared to commit a significant amount of my time, energy and resources to helping this happen.
The way I see it, sooooo many of our problems and conflicts are caused by a combination of

  1. A lack of connection to ourselves
  2. A lack of connection to others
  3. A lack of connection to the earth

So I feel like one of the most important things I can do in the world is to support and enable these forms of connection. And funnily enough, I LOVE doing this. Like it makes my heart soar. It 100% puts me into a state of flow, and doing the exercises from the book was what actually made me realise that. And it's been really great to sit with that and reflect on what is actually practically important to me.

Finding the strength to follow your dreams

As a generalisation, I think we live in a fear-based society. We are part of an economy that functions out of a scarcity-based mindset. And this teaches us to be conservative, to feel insecure, to second-guess ourselves. I have thoroughly enjoyed the journey of unlearning all of this, and realising how tangibly possible it actually is to chase all of our dreams. All of them! I started crafting a positive mindset at the age of 17 after reading The Secret. Because of my spiritual disposition I have been learning how to respond to all manner of emotions with curiosity and compassion for a wee while now. And for me inner calm is a matter of trust and acceptance, knowing that the both the rainy days and the sunny days will pass, and learning to stay in touch with the part of me that remains present through all of them.
