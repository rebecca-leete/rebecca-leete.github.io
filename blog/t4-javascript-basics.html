Javascript compared to HTML & CSS

Wow! I finally hit javascript! Or, really, it hit me. It's an interesting one, and as such an intuitive thinker I found it waaay harder to get my head around. I haven't done maths like that since NCEA level 2. It feels much more like I'm speaking directly to a computer than html or css, which I guess makes sense given the increased level of involvement between the computer and the commands I'm writing. I guess the main difference I see is that html and css languages are mostly descriptive in nature, whereas js is strictly logic and mathematics.

Flow and loops

Control flow is a process that regulates the performance of a certain action or set of actions depending on the conditions of a situation before, during and after it plays out.

Following? Probably not.

Let's look at it this way:
Think about having to refuel your car. Before you can do anything, you have to check: does my car need re-fuelling? (that's assessing the situation.) If your car has enough petrol, the conditions are not met for the action of refuelling your car to take place. If your car doesn't have enough petrol, you perform that action, until there is enough petrol in your car, and then you stop. And then when the conditions are right once again for your car to be refuelled (when it is empty), the process will be repeated all over again.
I hope that makes more sense...

Actually I think I've managed to merge control flow and looping into one example there... So just to clarify:

  • Control flow is the process of assessing a situation and deciding what action to take based on the conditions of a situation: refuel car if (car is empty); else (continue driving your car);
  • Looping is the process of repeating an action until you reach a defined situation: repeat pumping petrol into your car until the tank is full of petrol.

Getting into Arrays and Objects

Arrays and objects are both ways to store data. There's multiple data forms such as numbers, strings (this is javascript for you - it defines a "word" as a connected string of characters. Why can't it just be a word.), boolean (true or false data), etc.

The difference between the two is that data stored in an array is in the form of a series of ordered values, one after the other. To access that data you have to refer to a certain position within that series.

  • EG. Here is your array: [2, 4, 6, 8], and if you wanted to access say, the number 6, you have to refer to it's place in the array (remembering computers start counting from 0, not 1). So your request would involve something like: array[2].

Objects, on the other hand, store data in pairs, one part being the "key" and the other being the "value." It's kind of like labelling.

  • So here, imagine we had an object resembling a flower. Its data could be formatted like:
    "Name": "buttercup";
    "Petals": 5;
    "Leaves": 2;
    "Stem": 1;
    "Colour": "yellow";
    Where the first part of the pair is the key, and the second is the value. To access any of the values in an object, you have to refer to the key.
