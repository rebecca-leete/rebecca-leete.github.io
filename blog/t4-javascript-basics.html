Flow and loops

Control flow is a process that regulates the performance of a certain action or set of actions depending on the conditions of a situation before, during and after it plays out.

Following? Probably not.

Let's look at it this way:
Think about having to refuel your car. Before you can do anything, you have to check: does my car need re-fuelling? (that's assessing the situation.) If your car has enough petrol, the conditions are not met for the action of refuelling your car to take place. If your car doesn't have enough petrol, you perform that action, until there is enough petrol in your car, and then you stop. And then when the conditions are right once again for your car to be refuelled (when it is empty), the process will be repeated all over again.
I hope that makes more sense...

Actually I think I've managed to merge control flow and looping into one example there... So just to clarify:

  • Control flow is the process of assessing a situation and deciding what action to take based on the conditions of a situation: refuel car if (car is empty); else (continue driving your car);
  • looping is the process of repeating an action until you reach a defined situation: repeat pumping petrol into your car until the tank is full of petrol.
