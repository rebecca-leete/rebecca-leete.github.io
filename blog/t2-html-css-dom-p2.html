Grid-based Design!

Grid-based design is simultaneously one of the basic and master tools of any web-dev. It's a way of splitting up the webpage into a series of rows, spanning the length of the page, and within these rows dividing the space into columns, effectively creating a chess board of options for you to lay out your content.

Why responsive webpages are gold

The extent and nature of the internet's accessibility has grown drastica lly in the last 10 years or so. No longer is it only reached through a chunky PC and noisy dial up. Here and now, people are connected to the in ternet almost continuously, through all sorts of devices - PCs, laptops, notebooks, tablets, smart phones. The great thing about responsive webpages is that they are designed to have flexible display options so that they look great and work well on so many different screen sizes. Responsive webpages are worth looking into because they enable the developer to choose which elements stay on the page and where, whilst maintaining a professional aesthetic and smooth user interface.

Semantic Structure

In html, as we know, we have tags to divide and define the different elements in a webpage. The word semantic, in linguistics (BA student hollaa), literal ly just means "meaning." It transfers pretty well in html - semantic structu re simply refers to the practice of organising your page elements by tags that actually define the content. Instead of using generic tags like div and class, we can use specific tags such as header, nav, footer, article, etc.
